Purpose & Compounding Logic
Purpose

We empower productivity across the value chain — from smallholder farmer to large-scale producer — by solving for seasonality, spoilage, and market access, while improving freshness, affordability, and value capture. Going from extractive to productive. Owning the Whole Foods. Making possible what was previously impossible for those starting from nothing.

The Compounding Logic

Each layer reinforces the next. Storage fills the hub. Energy makes it resilient and currency-proof. Logistics creates the network. Data builds the moat. Export captures global value. SaaS above 15% of revenue triggers a multiple re-rate from 7× infrastructure to 10–12× platform — worth $25–40M on Year 5 EBITDA alone.

Design intent: The hub is designed at the intersection of three world-class cold storage references — NewCold’s vertical storage density and dark-warehouse energy philosophy, Kool-Ex Nairobi’s tropical-climate specification and pharma GDP zone model, and Lineage Logistics’ dock design and energy management practice. Every design decision is made to function reliably at 38–42°C ambient temperature, to meet NAFDAC GDP certification requirements for the pharmaceutical zone, and to be expanded to Phase 2 without structural modification to the Phase 1 building.
World-class design references

NewCold

Netherlands / Global  ·  World’s 3rd-largest refrigerated operator

High-bay automated, vertical storage density, dark-warehouse design (no human in cold zone routinely), 35–40% less energy than conventional equivalent per pallet stored.

Key borrow: Zone segregation, automation-ready aisle widths (3.6m), dark-warehouse LED motion sensor protocol, battery room maintained at +20°C to extend cell life.

Kool-Ex Cold Chain

Nairobi, Kenya  ·  Most comparable emerging market operator

4,000-pallet capacity, solar-powered, tropical climate design, pharmaceutical GDP zone. Operational and profitable. East Africa is 3–5 years ahead of West Africa in cold chain infrastructure development.

Key borrow: 120mm+ PIR panel spec for tropical humidity, GDP zone as enclosed room-within-room, multi-client SLA model, vapour barrier specification at 60–90% RH.

Lineage Logistics

US / Global  ·  World’s largest cold chain operator (S-1 2024)

Best-in-class energy management, dock design, and pallet density. Variable-speed compressors, waste heat recovery, lights-out operation, comprehensive IoT sensor networks.

Key borrow: Dock bay design with levellers, energy monitoring architecture, pallet density targets, IoT sensor placement protocol at zone transitions.

Design principles

Building envelope & insulation

  • 120mm PIR insulated panel minimum — Nigeria tropical spec (100mm is temperate standard, insufficient at 42°C ambient differential)
  • Long axis runs E–W: long north/south walls minimise afternoon solar gain on primary storage wall faces
  • Reflective white TPO/PVC roof membrane reduces radiant heat load by 25–35% vs dark roof at Lagos solar irradiance
  • Vapour barrier on the warm face of every panel — critical at Lagos humidity 60–90% RH, harmattan dust sealing also required
  • Raised heated floor slab in frozen zone — prevents ground heave from sustained −18°C soil temperature
  • Zero windows in any cold zone. Every zone transition has a double-door airlock vestibule — reduces heat ingress by 60%+ vs direct-access doors

Refrigeration & energy systems

  • Variable-speed compressors throughout — 30–40% energy saving at part-load vs fixed-speed (critical: hub rarely runs at full load in early years)
  • Distributed evaporator coils in every zone — no single point of failure; any coil can be isolated without zone shutdown
  • Ammonia/HFC hybrid: ammonia primary plant room, HFC in GDP pharma zone (ammonia excluded from pharma for regulatory compliance)
  • Waste heat from compressors routed to pre-cooling vestibule and hot water for welfare facilities — heat recovery reduces OPEX
  • LEDs on motion sensors only inside cold zones — ‘dark warehouse’ design reduces internal heat gain and electricity cost
  • Battery room climate-controlled at +20°C constant — extends lithium cell cycle life by 40%+ vs ambient storage in Lagos heat

Material flow & movement of goods

  • One-way truck flow west→east across north apron — eliminates reversing conflicts at dock, reduces accident risk
  • Produce enters south face → processing → cold storage (flows north) — linear flow with no product crossing pathways
  • Separate dock levellers and airlocks per temperature zone — prevents zone contamination and temperature bleed between zones
  • 3.6m minimum aisle width throughout — reach truck plus passing clearance, meets AS/RS automation retrofit specification
  • Drive-in racking: 3 pallets deep, 5 high — FIFO with depth lanes, designed for AS/RS retrofit from Phase 2 without racking replacement
  • Fixed UHF RFID reader at every dock gate and every zone transition — chain-of-custody recorded at each physical handoff point

Expansion & future-proofing

  • Phase 2 expansion zone reserved west of building — 50m × 40m = 2,000 sqm connects directly to existing cool and processing zones via new airlock panels
  • Plant room oversized 20% at build — spare compressor slots and inverter capacity pre-installed; no plant room expansion required for Phase 2
  • Battery room conduit pre-run to western expansion boundary during original construction — saves significant retrofit cost
  • AS/RS automation pre-designed: floor loading at 6 T/sqm and 3.6m aisle width meet full automation requirements throughout
  • Modular cold room panel construction — any zone can be subdivided, enlarged, or re-temperatured at relatively low cost
  • NERC surplus energy connection sized for combined Phase 1+2 load from day one — no metering upgrade at Phase 2
Pre-construction validation checklist

The following items require field validation and professional sign-off before any capital commitment or construction drawings are finalised.

Engage Thermosystems Nigeria or Frigoglass for cold room construction quotation and insulation spec sign-off — confirm 120mm vs 150mm PIR for frozen zone at 42°C ambient differential
Commission Arnergy Solar or Daystar Power for budgetary EPC quote: 750kW PV + 1,500kWh BESS, all-in installed cost, degradation warranty in tropical climate

Critical Risks — Threaten Business Viability
Critical
FX & Currency Volatility
 Risk: Naira has lost 70%+ against USD in three years. DFI debt, equipment replacement, and investor IRR all USD-denominated. Sharp devaluation widens the gap faster than the model can absorb. Mitigation: Solar-first design eliminates the largest naira cost (energy). 30–40% of revenue architected as USD-indexed — export services, RFID SaaS, AI grading. Natural hedge: naira opex falls in dollar terms when naira weakens.
Critical
Logistics Theft & Hijacking
 Risk: Armed robbery of refrigerated trucks on Nigerian highways is documented and recurring — Lagos–Ibadan, Kano–Kaduna, Southeast routes. A single hijacking destroys cargo, vehicle, potentially a driver. Mitigation: Route risk assessment, curfew protocols, no night driving in high-risk corridors. Real-time GPS geofencing. Armed escort for pharma loads. Cargo insurance from day one. Driver non-resistance training.
Critical
Power Infrastructure Failure
 Risk: Inverter faults and battery degradation can create temperature excursions. In 38–42°C ambient, a 4-hour power failure in a cold room is a total produce loss event. Mitigation: Redundant design: solar primary, battery secondary, diesel generator tertiary. Auto transfer switch <30 second failover. 24/7 monitoring, SMS alerts. Maintenance SLA with refrigeration OEM.
Critical
Cofounder Gap
 Risk: The single most common failure mode in African infrastructure plays — visionary founder, wrong or absent operational cofounder. Every assumption is a single point of failure until the operations seat is filled. Mitigation: Non-negotiable condition: no Phase 1 capital deployment without cofounder in seat. Use Phase 0 truck programme as a live test — cofounder should be running it, not observing it.
High Risks — Material Impact on Timeline or Returns
High
Hub Underutilisation
 An LOI is not a volume commitment. Anchor clients reduce bookings during slow seasons without penalty if contract is poorly structured. Minimum monthly volume clause in every SLA. Tiered pricing rewards consistent commitment. Do not break ground without signed minimum-volume contract.
High
Anchor Client Concentration
 One or two clients representing 60%+ of hub revenue. An exit or volume reduction puts the hub below breakeven — structural risk in the early model. Minimum volume clauses + penalties. Target 4–5 anchors before 80% utilisation. No single client above 35% of revenue. Diversify: supermarket + processor + pharma + cooperative blocks.
High
Regulatory & Licensing Delays
 NAFDAC pharma accreditation, NERC surplus licence, Lagos State planning consent can each take 12–24 months and involve informal facilitation demands. Engage specialist regulatory counsel before site selection. Map and file all licences in parallel. Budget 12% hard CapEx contingency. NIRSAL partnership accelerates federal alignment.
High
Talent Scarcity — Cold Chain Technicians
 Nigeria has a critical shortage of trained refrigeration engineers. A compressor failure with no qualified technician within 4 hours is a product loss event. Partner with refrigeration OEM for training and certification programme. Retain two trained technicians per hub. Preventive maintenance schedule to reduce reactive repair frequency.
High
Fuel Price Volatility
 Post-subsidy diesel price volatility is severe and unpredictable. A 40–60% diesel spike directly erodes logistics margins and hub backup costs. Solar-first limits diesel to backup use only. CNG/LPG fleet retrofit reduces diesel dependency. Logistics pricing includes fuel escalation clause tied to published pump prices.
Medium Risks — Manageable with Early Action
Medium
Behavioural Adoption Lag
 Smallholder farmers and market traders have adapted to spoilage as accepted loss. Changing post-harvest behaviour takes 3–5× longer than projected. Do not model farmer/cooperative volumes as anchor revenue in Year 1. Institutional B2B carries the hub economics. Farmer adoption is the social impact story and Year 3+ uplift.
Medium
Solar Degradation & Battery Replacement
 In Nigerian heat, humidity, and harmattan dust, lithium cells degrade faster than temperate benchmarks. A capacity shortfall at Year 5 is a capital event the model may understate. Annual battery capacity testing from Year 2. Budget battery replacement reserve from Year 3 as sinking fund. Specify tropical climate ratings. 10-year performance warranty from EPC partner.
Medium
Data Platform Adoption
 Enterprise clients have IT resistance, legacy systems, procurement bureaucracy. Low portal adoption limits the data moat and delays SaaS revenue. Design for zero-integration — browser-based, no API required. First 90 days portal access at no cost builds habit. RFID tagging at hub gate regardless of portal adoption — data accumulates either way.
